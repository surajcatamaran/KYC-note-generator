Research & Sourcing
- Research from the company website, quarterly presentation, annual report, investor presentations, earnings call transcripts (available on screener.com), exchange filings. 
-include a specific source for every fact (presentation slide #, AR page #, website section URL, filing date/link, news outlet/date). Use the PDF screenshot tool for ARs/filings. If images help, use image search and include an image carousel. for the sources, just put a number (e.g. 1, 2, etc. near the data point and then hyperlink it to source at the end of the note, where you should create a section of sources)
- For listed Indian companies, use screener.com (earnings data, P&L, balance sheet, shareholding). For global listed companies, use Yahoo Finance or SEC/EDGAR. For Indian private companies, use Tracxn or Probe. 
- Never guess. If a figure is unavailable in public sources, state “Not disclosed publicly” and proceed. 

Structure and Content (tailor to company specifics)

1) Company Overview (pointed, not texty)
- Source order: first check latest quarterly presentation; if missing, then the latest annual report.
- Output should be in the following format:
  • The company ‘XX’ was founded in <year>. It was founded by <name>. 
  • It is headquartered in <city, country>.
  • The company has <#> manufacturing plants across <locations> (if applicable).
  • It has <#> employees (from AR: total incl. contract employees if disclosed).
•	The founder is xx engineer / CA / etc. – explain the founder’s background (engineer/technocrat/etc.) and also tell where did he/she do their education from and in which year

- Then: a narrative history in “simple story format”: founding (year, place, founder background—engineer/technocrat/etc.), initial product/service, chronological evolution with key phases, exports start (where/when), pivots, capacity expansions, technological partnerships/JVs, acquisitions/mergers, leadership transitions (founder → professional CEO: when/why), current CEO profile (education, prior employers, from LinkedIn/news/AR), up to present day. Prefer slides from investor deck; else AR; else credible media/sell-side. This is important instruction – “For getting the story and history of the company, you can refer to interviews or historical videos on youtube.com and you can refer https://forum.valuepickr.com/ and refer to quarterly presentation slides or annual report where they talk about evolution”
	
- Auditors, bankers, and major shareholders: 
  • Auditors & bankers from AR only (list names exactly as disclosed; do not infer).
  • Major shareholders from bseindia.com or screener.com (top FIIs/DIIs/public if disclosed). Keep this pointed.

2) Offerings and Products/Services (simple language, detailed)
- For each product/service: what it is; what it does; how it’s made (if manufacturing, outline process steps at a high level); or how service is delivered (if services company). 

- Then explain how difficult or easy is it to manufacture this product or service. And why? (you can give references for e.g., that this product is manufactured by only 8-10 companies globally, hence this is a consolidated industry, because this is a difficult product and so on). This section is important, so do it diligently and accurately
- Include revenue mix by segment/product/geography if disclosed (cite source and period). 
- Identify end customers/industries and where the product fits in the customer’s system/operation; explain the functional/technical role. 

3) Industry and Market (pointed and specific)
- Define industry/sub-segments. 
- Market position/market share in home geography and/or globally (cite only if from reliable sources; otherwise omit share figures). 
- Main competitors: list domestic and global separately. Include market shares only with reliable sourcing. Is it a concentrated market or are there numerous players in this market

4) Customers and Use Cases
- List notable customers; categorize by geography/segment. Use presentations, AR, websites, case studies, LinkedIn, sell-side. 
- For B2B: usage scenarios—how customers use the product. For B2C: target demographics.

5) Financials. For listed companies in India, take the data only from screener.com. Don’t make up your own numbers. Just take numbers for a given company as is from their website for the past 5 years and TTM (trailing twelve months). Operating profit on screener.com means EBITDA.
The table should contain the below
- revenue, EBITDA, EBITDA margin, PAT, and growth rates for at least the past 3–5 years. 
- Provide segment/geography/product splits if disclosed. 
- Data sources: 
  • India (listed): screener.com 
  • India (private): Tracxn/Probe. 
  • Global (listed): Yahoo Finance or SEC filings (10-K/20-F/10-Q). 
- Present data in neat tables. 
- If available, add a table of 2–3 year revenue and PAT forecasts from top sell-side (e.g., JPM, Bernstein, CLSA, Investec, Spark, Motilal Oswal, Kotak, HDFC, ICICI, Emkay, Anand Rathi, B&K). Cite report/date. If paywalled, cite summary references from credible news/exchange filings.

6) Management and Governance
- Board of Directors and Senior Management tables with concise fields: Name | Role | Age | Education (degree + college/university) | Past Employers (list names only) | Joined (year) | Other Directorships (for BoD). 
- Pull from AR, company website, LinkedIn, credible news; consolidate. Keep crisp—no long paragraphs.
Keep it to the point and don’t make it texty

7) Visuals and Diagrams
- Show key offerings and where they sit in systems/value chains. Provide short, clear captions explaining each visual.

8) Recent Developments and Strategic Initiatives (brief and pointed)
- M&A, JVs, partnerships, capacity/geography/talent expansions. 
- Product launches, tech upgrades. 
- Regulatory/macro events impacting performance. 
- Leadership hires: new CEO/CXO searches/appointments tied to turnaround or scale-up.
